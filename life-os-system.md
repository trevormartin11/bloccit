# Life OS — The System

Built from the 100-question discovery analysis. This is the operating system — not aspirational, but executable. Every protocol here is designed for one person with CIRS, a 6-week kambo window cycle, willpower that fluctuates with health, and a proven track record of performing when structure exists.

---

## 1. Operating Principles

These are the rules the entire system is built on. When in doubt, defer to these.

1. **Health is infrastructure, not a goal.** Running and sleep are not things you "try to get to." They are the foundation that makes everything else possible. Skipping them doesn't save time — it destroys days.

2. **Never negotiate with yourself in the moment.** The decision was made last night during planning. Today you execute. If the plan says run, you run. The only acceptable override is a genuine health crisis — not "I don't feel like it."

3. **Defaults over decisions.** Every recurring choice (what to eat, when to work, what to do next) should have a pre-set default. Willpower is a finite resource. Spend it on things that matter, not on deciding what's for dinner.

4. **Protect the peak.** 8:30–11:00 AM is sacred. Nothing low-value gets in. Not email, not in-law calls, not errands. This is where income is generated.

5. **Survive the dips.** The system has two modes. When health crashes, the goal isn't productivity — it's preventing total collapse so you don't rebuild from zero.

6. **Track the lead indicators, not just results.** Did you run? Did you plan? Did you protect the morning block? These predict everything downstream. Income and body composition are lagging indicators — they follow the habits.

7. **80% execution beats 100% planning.** A good-enough system you actually follow is infinitely better than a perfect system you abandon after a week.

## 2. Two-Mode Architecture

Your CIRS creates a reality most productivity systems ignore: you have windows of high function and windows of low function, and you can't fully control which one you're in. The system must account for both.

### Mode A: Healthy Window (Currently Active — Post-Kambo)

Full protocol. This is where you build momentum, generate income, train hard, and push forward. The goal during healthy windows is to **bank as much progress as possible** and **lock in habits deeply enough that they partially survive the dip.**

### Mode B: Symptomatic Mode

Minimum viable protocol. The goal is NOT productivity. It's:
- Preserve the 3 habits that prevent total collapse (movement, planning, sleep hygiene)
- Maintain basic nutrition (default meals, no decisions)
- Do 1 hour of highest-priority work only
- Prevent the guilt spiral ("I'm behind on everything") from compounding the symptoms

**How to know which mode you're in:** If you wake up and your brain fog is bad enough that reading a paragraph feels hard, or your fatigue is bad enough that the idea of running feels physically impossible — you're in Mode B. Don't fight it. Run the Mode B protocol. A Mode B day executed well is worth 10x more than a Mode A day you failed at because you were pretending to be healthy.

## 8. Financial System

### Why Previous Budgets Failed

You tried to build a habit of daily/weekly manual expense tracking. Five times. It failed every time because:
1. Manual input is tedious
2. You have variable income which makes traditional budgets feel pointless
3. When health dips, it's the first thing to go
4. Automated tools didn't categorize well enough

### The New Approach: Containment, Not Tracking

Instead of tracking every dollar, **contain spending by design** so the damage is limited even without tracking.

### Step 1: The Two-Card System

| Card | Purpose | Monthly Limit/Budget |
|---|---|---|
| Card A — "Fixed" | All recurring bills, subscriptions, insurance, debt payments | ~$10k (auto-pay everything) |
| Card B — "Life" | All discretionary spending: food, DoorDash, shopping, gas, entertainment | Hard cap: $3,000/month |

**Why this works:** You don't need to track daily. You just check one number once a week: "How much is left on Card B this month?" If it's getting low, you slow down. If it's fine, keep going. That's the entire system.

### Step 2: Friday Financial Check (5 Minutes)

Every Friday during your admin block:

1. Open Card B statement → check spend-to-date this month (30 seconds)
2. Open Card A statement → confirm no surprises (30 seconds)
3. Check bank balance → is it above $10k floor? (30 seconds)
4. Log one number in a simple spreadsheet or note: "Week of [date]: Card B at $X of $3,000" (1 minute)
5. Done.

If spend is trending over, you know by week 2 or 3 and can adjust. No daily logging. No categorization. Just one number.

### Step 3: Post-Durango Sale Plan

When the Durango house sells (~$400–500k equity):

1. **Pay off ALL credit card debt** (~$100k) — immediately. No negotiation. This eliminates the highest-interest liability and frees up significant monthly cash flow.
2. **Pay off family office loan** (if possible with remaining proceeds) — removes the financial entanglement with in-laws, which has both financial and emotional value.
3. **Establish $30k emergency fund** — 2 months of expenses. This is the first time you'll have a real cushion.
4. **Remaining funds → pay down primary mortgage or invest** — discuss with a fee-only financial advisor (not the in-laws' people).

### Step 4: Income Tracking

Bill every hour you work. Every single one. You said you're not great at this — that's money you're leaving on the table.

**Rule: At the end of every AM and PM work block, log your hours.** Takes 60 seconds. Don't batch it to the end of the week — you'll forget. Log it when you stop working.

At $75/hr, every unbilled hour is $75 gone. If you miss 5 hours a week (very possible if you're not tracking), that's $375/week = $1,625/month = $19,500/year. That's a real number.

## 10. Accountability Architecture

### Why Coaches Failed

You said coaches "don't provide the level of accountability I need." Unpacking that: most coaches do a weekly call and check in on goals. That's not frequent enough for someone whose habits erode gradually. By the time a weekly check-in catches the erosion, you've already lost 5 days.

### What You Actually Need: Daily Micro-Accountability

**The Daily Scorecard (2 minutes, done during nightly plan)**

Score yourself on 7 binary questions. Yes or No. No partial credit.

| # | Question | Y/N |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Did I run today (+ cold plunge in Mode A)? | |
| 2 | Did I protect the AM block for high-value work? | |
| 3 | Did I eat the default meals (no unplanned DoorDash, no wheat)? | |
| 4 | Did I bill all my hours? | |
| 5 | Did I do sauna + red light therapy? | |
| 6 | Did I brush my teeth? | |
| 7 | Is my phone outside the bedroom right now? | |

**Scoring:**
- 7/7 = Perfect day. These stack.
- 6/7 = Good day. Note which one you missed and why.
- 5/7 = Slipping. Tomorrow must be 6+.
- 4/7 or below = Emergency. You're in or approaching a dip. Consider switching to Mode B.

**Track it visually.** A whiteboard in your office, a simple paper tracker, or a single-column spreadsheet. The streak becomes its own motivation — "I've hit 4+ for 14 days straight" creates momentum that willpower alone can't.

### Weekly Accountability: The Wife Check-In

Your marriage is 10/10. Your wife is your partner. Use that.

**Sunday evening, 10 minutes, during or after the weekly review:**
- Share your scorecard for the week (how many 5/5, 4/5, etc.)
- Share the financial number (Card B spend)
- Share next week's top 3 priorities

This isn't about her being your coach. It's about saying it out loud to someone you respect. You said fear of consequences motivates you — well, the consequence of not doing it is having to look your wife in the eye and say "I didn't."

### The Anti-Erosion Mechanism

You said your systems erode gradually, not all at once. That means you need an early warning system.

**The 2-Day Rule:** If you score below 6/7 for two consecutive days, you must do ONE of the following before going to sleep:
1. Write down (on paper, not in your head) what specifically caused the miss
2. Identify if you're entering a symptomatic period (if so, switch to Mode B — no shame)
3. Set an alarm 30 minutes earlier tomorrow

This prevents the "I missed today, I'll do better tomorrow" loop from becoming a 2-week slide.
