# Garmin Xero Angle Compensation Research

## Question
Does the Garmin Xero compensate for angles, such as shooting directly down at an animal from an elevated position?

## Answer
Yes. The Garmin Xero bow sights automatically compensate for shot angle (uphill or downhill) using angle-compensated ranging.

### How It Works
1. The built-in laser rangefinder measures the line-of-sight distance to the target.
2. An internal inclinometer measures the angle of the shot.
3. The sight calculates the true horizontal (gravity-effective) distance.
4. A single LED aiming pin is projected at the correct position for that compensated distance.

### Why Angle Compensation Matters
When shooting at a steep angle (e.g., from a treestand directly down at game), the line-of-sight distance is longer than the effective ballistic distance. Without compensation, the archer would aim for too great a distance and shoot high. The Xero eliminates this error automatically.

### Third Axis Adjustment
The Xero also includes Third Axis Adjustment, which further improves accuracy at severe angles over longer distances — critical for treestand hunting or steep terrain.

### Specifications
- Range: up to 100 yards on game, 300 yards on reflective targets
- Supports up to 10 different arrow profiles
- Ambient light sensor optimizes pin brightness

### Models with Angle Compensation
- Xero A1 / A1i (compound bow)
- Xero A1i Pro (compound bow, latest version)
- Xero X1i (crossbow, supports speeds up to 650 FPS)

### Regulatory Note
Some jurisdictions regulate or prohibit electronic bow sights for hunting. Always verify local regulations before use.
