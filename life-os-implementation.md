## 1. Tonight: Setup Checklist

Before anything else works, you need to set up your environment. Do these once, tonight.

### Physical Setup

- [ ] **Find a phone charging spot outside the bedroom.** Kitchen counter, hallway outlet, office — anywhere that requires you to physically get out of bed to reach it. Plug the charger in now.
- [ ] **Set tomorrow's alarm on your phone.** 6:30 AM. Loud. Place the phone on the charger outside the bedroom.
- [ ] **Lay out running clothes.** Shorts, shirt, socks, shoes — set them in the bathroom or next to your bed. Include the chest strap HR monitor.
- [ ] **Put a notebook and pen on your nightstand** (or wherever you'll do the nightly plan). A cheap spiral notebook is perfect. Don't use an app — the phone is outside the bedroom.
- [ ] **Check the fridge:** Do you have chicken and sweet potatoes for tomorrow? If not, add them to a grocery order right now. You need both by tomorrow afternoon.

### Recovery Setup

- [ ] **Cold plunge setup.** Cold plunge tub, stock tank, or ice bath — whatever you have access to. Fill it and verify the temperature (target: 38–50°F). If you don't have a setup yet, a cold shower at the coldest setting works as a starting point while you get one.
- [ ] **Sauna ready to go.** Confirm it's operational and you can have it heated by 5:00 PM tomorrow. If infrared, typical heat-up time is 15–20 minutes — plan accordingly.
- [ ] **Red light therapy panel positioned.** Set it up in or near the sauna so you can run both simultaneously. If using a portable panel, place it where it hits your torso/face during the session.

### Digital Setup

- [ ] **Set your phone to Do Not Disturb** from 6:30 AM–11:00 AM, auto-schedule it so it happens every weekday without you thinking about it.
- [ ] **Download Strava** (if not already installed). Create an account. Pair your chest strap HR monitor.
- [ ] **Create a simple spreadsheet or note** titled "Weekly Spend." One column: Date. Second column: Card B balance. That's it.

### Financial Setup (Do This Week, Not Tonight)

- [ ] **Identify which credit card will be Card A (fixed expenses)** and which will be **Card B (discretionary).** If you only have one card, get a second one this week — or use your debit card as Card B.
- [ ] **Set up auto-pay** on Card A for all recurring bills (mortgage, insurance, subscriptions, utilities, debt minimums).
- [ ] **Set a spend alert** on Card B at $2,250 (75% of $3,000 monthly cap). Most card apps let you do this in settings.

## 2. The Nightly Plan — Exact Template

**When:** 9:30 PM, after your phone goes on the charger outside the bedroom.
**Where:** Notebook on your nightstand or desk.
**Time required:** 8–10 minutes.

### What You Write

Copy this format every night. It fits on one notebook page.

```
DATE: ___________

TOMORROW'S SCHEDULE:
  Wake: 6:30
  Run: 6:45–7:45 → [distance target: ___ miles]
  AM Block (8:30–11):
    Task 1: ________________________________
    Task 2 (if time): ________________________________
  Midday (11:30–1:30):
    ________________________________
  Meal 1: 1:30 (chicken + sweet potato — prepped? Y/N)
  PM Block / Gym: 2:30–4:00
    ________________________________
  Evening meal default: [protein ice cream / yogurt bowl / leftovers]

NOTES:
  Anything on my mind that I need to dump so I can sleep:
  ________________________________
```

### Rules for the Nightly Plan

1. **Task 1 in the AM block must be revenue-generating.** ReplyScale outreach, real estate deal work, or the single most valuable thing you could do tomorrow. Not email. Not admin.
2. **The meal plan must be filled in.** "I'll figure it out" is not an answer. If Meal 1 isn't prepped, write "PREP CHICKEN IN MORNING" as a note.
3. **The notes section is for brain dumping.** If something's rattling around your head — write it down. It doesn't need a solution. Getting it on paper lets you sleep.
4. **Don't over-plan.** 2 tasks in the AM block is plenty. If you finish both, pull another from your list. But starting with 6 things guarantees you'll feel behind by 10 AM.

### What Input Is Required From You

- 8 minutes of writing
- Deciding your #1 revenue task for tomorrow (this should take <60 seconds — it's usually obvious)
- Checking if food is prepped

## 3. The Daily Scorecard — Exact Template

**When:** During nightly plan, as the last thing you write before closing the notebook.
**Time required:** 2 minutes.

### What You Write

Add this to the bottom of each nightly plan page:

```
TODAY'S SCORE:
  [  ] Ran today (+ cold plunge in Mode A)
  [  ] AM block protected (no email/calls before 11)
  [  ] Default meals (no unplanned DoorDash, no wheat)
  [  ] Billed all hours
  [  ] Sauna + red light therapy
  [  ] Phone outside bedroom right now

  Score: ___/6

  If below 5: What specifically happened?
  ________________________________
```

### How to Score

- **Check the box** only if it fully happened. Partial credit = no check.
- "Ran today (+ cold plunge)" counts if you ran at least 20 minutes (on Mode A days) AND did the cold plunge after. A walk does NOT count for Mode A scoring. In Mode B, a 20–30 minute walk counts (no cold plunge required).
- "AM block protected" means you did NOT check email, take calls, or do admin work between 8:30 and 11:00. If you checked your phone once for 2 minutes — still counts. If you spent 30 minutes on email — doesn't count.
- "Default meals" means you ate the planned meals. One DoorDash order = no check. One wheat-containing food = no check.
- "Billed all hours" means you logged time at the end of each work block today, and no hours are unaccounted for.
- "Sauna + red light" means you did the full 25-minute sauna session with red light therapy. This counts in BOTH modes. No partial credit — 10 minutes doesn't count.
- "Phone outside bedroom" — it's either there or it's not.

### The 2-Day Rule

If you score 4/6 or below for **two consecutive days**, you must write the answer to this question before going to sleep:

> "Am I entering a symptomatic period? If yes → switch to Mode B tomorrow. If no → what specifically will I change tomorrow to get back to 4+?"

## 4. Running Program — Full 12-Week Plan

### Starting Point Assessment

Before you start, run 1 mile at a comfortable pace. Note:
- Your time
- Your average heart rate (from chest strap)
- How you felt at the end (1–10)

This tells you where to start in the program below.

### Heart Rate Zones

With a chest strap, you'll train by heart rate. Estimate your max HR at **220 minus your age** (adjust if you know yours from testing). For age 31: **Max HR ≈ 189 bpm.**

| Zone | % of Max HR | BPM Range | Feel | When to Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Zone 1 | 50–60% | 95–113 | Very easy, can hold full conversation | Warm-up, cool-down |
| Zone 2 | 60–70% | 113–132 | Easy, can talk in full sentences | Most of your running (base building) |
| Zone 3 | 70–80% | 132–151 | Moderate, can talk in short phrases | Tempo portions |
| Zone 4 | 80–90% | 151–170 | Hard, a few words at a time | Intervals (later in program) |

### The Key Insight: Most of Your Running Should Be SLOW

Zone 2 running builds your aerobic base, burns fat efficiently, and is sustainable. It should feel embarrassingly easy. If you're gasping, you're going too fast. **80% of your weekly miles should be in Zone 2.**

### Weeks 1–4: Base Building

**Goal:** Build the habit, build your aerobic base, don't get injured at 240 lbs.

| Day | Workout | Target Zone | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | 2.5 miles easy | Zone 2 | If you can't run the full distance, walk/run intervals: run 3 min, walk 1 min |
| Tue | 2.5 miles easy | Zone 2 | Same pace as Monday |
| Wed | 3 miles easy | Zone 2 | Slightly longer |
| Thu | 2.5 miles easy | Zone 2 | Easy effort |
| Fri | 3 miles easy | Zone 2 | |
| Sat | 3.5 miles easy | Zone 2 | Long run day — go SLOW |
| Sun | Rest or 20-min walk | — | Recovery |

**Weekly total: ~17 miles**

**If 2.5 miles is too hard right now:** Start with 1.5 miles (run/walk is fine) and add 0.25 miles per run until you reach the targets above. There is no shame in starting where you are. The system only works if you don't get injured in week 2.

### Weeks 5–8: Building Distance

**Goal:** Get to 5 miles comfortably. Introduce one slightly harder effort per week.

| Day | Workout | Target Zone | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | 3.5 miles easy | Zone 2 | |
| Tue | 3 miles with 4×30-sec pickups | Zone 2, pickups in Zone 3 | Run easy, then 30 seconds faster, then easy again. 2 min easy between pickups. |
| Wed | 4 miles easy | Zone 2 | |
| Thu | 3.5 miles easy | Zone 2 | |
| Fri | 4 miles easy | Zone 2 | |
| Sat | 5 miles easy | Zone 2 | Long run |
| Sun | Rest or walk | — | |

**Weekly total: ~23 miles**

### Weeks 9–12: Performance Building

**Goal:** Solidify 5-mile daily runs. One tempo run per week for fitness gains.

| Day | Workout | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | 5 miles easy (Zone 2) | |
| Tue | 4 miles with tempo: 1 mi easy, 2 mi at Zone 3, 1 mi easy | The tempo portion should feel "comfortably hard" — you can say a few words but not hold a conversation |
| Wed | 5 miles easy (Zone 2) | |
| Thu | 4 miles easy (Zone 2) | Lighter day before Friday |
| Fri | 5 miles easy (Zone 2) | |
| Sat | 6–7 miles easy (Zone 2) | Long run |
| Sun | Rest or walk | |

**Weekly total: ~29–31 miles**

### Running Rules (All Weeks)

1. **If you feel a sharp pain (not soreness, PAIN), stop.** Walk home. Take a rest day. Run again when pain-free. Running through injury at 240 lbs is how you lose months.
2. **Stretch after every run.** 5 minutes minimum. Quads, hamstrings, calves, hip flexors. Standing stretches while cooling down are fine.
3. **Hydrate before the run.** 16 oz of water with your matcha. Don't run dehydrated.
4. **Cold plunge immediately after the run (3–5 minutes).** Get in while your body is still warm from the run. Start at 2 minutes if you're new to it, build to 5 minutes over a few weeks. Then shower. The run + cold plunge combo maximizes the anti-inflammatory and dopamine response.
5. **Log every run in Strava.** Start the GPS before you leave. Stop it when you're done. This is non-negotiable — the data is your accountability.
6. **Bad-day minimum: 20 minutes.** If you absolutely cannot do the programmed distance, just run for 20 minutes at whatever pace. The streak > the workout.

### What Input Is Required From You

- Show up and run at the prescribed effort
- Hit start/stop on Strava
- Note your distance and average HR (Strava does this automatically)
- If using run/walk intervals in early weeks, set a repeating timer (3 min run / 1 min walk)

## 5. Lifting Program — Full Program with Every Exercise

### Program: Upper/Lower/Push-Pull/Full — 4 Days

This is a straightforward hypertrophy + strength program. Compound movements, progressive overload, nothing exotic. Every exercise is chosen for a reason.

### How Progressive Overload Works

This is the ONLY thing that matters for building muscle and strength.

**The rule:** When you can complete all prescribed sets and reps with good form, **add 5 lbs to the bar next session** (upper body) or **10 lbs** (lower body).

If you fail to complete all reps at a weight, use the same weight next session and try again. Three sessions at the same weight with no progress = drop 10% and build back up.

**Log every session.** Use a notes app, a gym notebook, or an app like Strong/Hevy/Heavy. Write: Exercise, Weight, Sets × Reps completed. Example: "Bench Press: 185 lbs, 4×8, 4×8, 4×7, 4×6" — that tells you next session to keep 185 until you hit 4×8 across all sets.

---

### Day 1: UPPER — PUSH FOCUS (Monday)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Rest | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Barbell Bench Press** | 4 × 6–8 | 2–3 min | Main strength movement. Full range of motion, bar to chest. |
| **Overhead Press (barbell or dumbbell)** | 3 × 8–10 | 2 min | Standing preferred. Brace your core. |
| **Incline Dumbbell Press** | 3 × 10–12 | 90 sec | 30-degree incline. Squeeze at the top. |
| **Lateral Raises** | 3 × 12–15 | 60 sec | Light weight, controlled. Don't swing. |
| **Tricep Pushdowns (cable or band)** | 3 × 12–15 | 60 sec | Full extension at the bottom. |
| **Face Pulls** | 3 × 15–20 | 60 sec | Shoulder health. Light weight, squeeze shoulder blades. |

**Total time: ~45–50 minutes**

---

### Day 2: LOWER BODY (Wednesday)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Rest | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Barbell Back Squat** | 4 × 6–8 | 3 min | Main strength movement. Hit parallel or below. If mobility is an issue, use a goblet squat until you can squat properly. |
| **Romanian Deadlift (barbell)** | 3 × 8–10 | 2 min | Hinge at hips, slight knee bend, feel the hamstring stretch. Bar stays close to legs. |
| **Walking Lunges (dumbbell)** | 3 × 10 each leg | 90 sec | Alternate legs. Upright torso. |
| **Leg Press** | 3 × 10–12 | 90 sec | Feet shoulder-width, full range. |
| **Calf Raises (machine or smith)** | 4 × 12–15 | 60 sec | Full stretch at bottom, pause at top. |
| **Plank** | 3 × 45–60 sec | 60 sec | Tight core, flat back. If easy, add weight on your back. |

**Total time: ~50–55 minutes**

---

### Day 3: UPPER — PULL FOCUS (Thursday)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Rest | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Barbell Row (or Pendlay Row)** | 4 × 6–8 | 2–3 min | Main pull movement. Squeeze shoulder blades at top. |
| **Pull-ups or Lat Pulldown** | 3 × 8–10 (or to failure if doing pull-ups) | 2 min | If you can't do 8 pull-ups, use the lat pulldown until you can. |
| **Seated Cable Row** | 3 × 10–12 | 90 sec | Chest up, squeeze at the contraction. |
| **Dumbbell Curl** | 3 × 10–12 | 60 sec | No swinging. Control the eccentric (lowering). |
| **Hammer Curl** | 3 × 10–12 | 60 sec | Neutral grip. Hits the brachialis for arm thickness. |
| **Rear Delt Fly (dumbbell or machine)** | 3 × 15–20 | 60 sec | Light weight. Shoulder health + posture. |

**Total time: ~45–50 minutes**

---

### Day 4: FULL BODY / LEGS EMPHASIS (Friday)

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Rest | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Conventional Deadlift** | 4 × 5 | 3 min | Main strength movement. Reset each rep from the floor. Brace hard. Don't round your lower back. |
| **Bulgarian Split Squat (dumbbell)** | 3 × 8–10 each leg | 90 sec | Rear foot on bench. This builds single-leg strength and balance. |
| **Dumbbell Bench Press** | 3 × 10–12 | 90 sec | Different stimulus than barbell. Full stretch at bottom. |
| **Cable Row or Dumbbell Row** | 3 × 10–12 | 90 sec | |
| **Leg Curl (machine)** | 3 × 12–15 | 60 sec | Hamstring isolation. |
| **Ab Wheel Rollout or Hanging Leg Raise** | 3 × 10–15 | 60 sec | If ab wheel is too hard from the floor, do it from your knees. |

**Total time: ~50–55 minutes**

---

### Saturday: OPTIONAL (Pick Anything You Enjoy)

If you feel good after the long run, you can hit the gym for 30 minutes. Do whatever you want — arms, machines, whatever is fun. This day exists to keep the gym habit alive, not to follow a strict program. If you don't feel like it, skip it.

---

### Warm-Up (Every Lifting Session — 5 Minutes)

Don't skip this. You're 240 lbs and your joints need it.

1. 3 minutes on a bike or rower (easy pace, just get blood flowing)
2. 10 arm circles each direction
3. 10 bodyweight squats
4. 10 band pull-aparts
5. 1 warm-up set of the first exercise at 50% working weight

### What Input Is Required From You

- Show up on the scheduled day
- Follow the exercise order as written
- Log every set: Exercise, Weight, Reps completed
- When you hit all prescribed reps across all sets → add weight next session
- Total gym time including warm-up: ~55–65 minutes

## 6. Nutrition — Exact Meals, Macros, and Prep Instructions

### Daily Macro Targets

For fat loss while preserving/building muscle at 240 lbs on TRT:

| Macro | Daily Target | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Protein | 200g minimum | 1g per lb of lean body mass (~192 lbs). Rounds up for simplicity. Muscle preservation during the cut. |
| Calories | ~2,200–2,500 | Moderate deficit. You're running 20–30 miles/week and lifting 4x — don't crash your metabolism. |
| Carbs | 150–200g | Fuel for running and lifting. Mostly from sweet potatoes. |
| Fat | 60–80g | Enough for hormone function (especially important with TRT). |

**You don't need to track macros daily.** The default meals below are designed to hit these targets automatically. If you eat these meals and nothing else, you're in the right range without logging a single thing.

### Meal 1: The Anchor (1:30–2:30 PM)

**What:**
- 8 oz chicken breast (weighed raw) — 50g protein, ~250 cal
- 500g sweet potato (weighed raw, about 2 medium potatoes) — 5g protein, ~430 cal, ~100g carbs
- Sriracha to taste — ~0 cal
- Optional: big handful of spinach (~30g) mixed in — 1g protein, ~7 cal

**Macro total: ~55g protein, ~680 cal, ~100g carbs, ~5g fat**

### Meal Prep Instructions for Meal 1

Do this **twice a week** — Sunday and Wednesday evening.

**Chicken:**
1. Buy chicken breast in bulk (3–4 lbs at a time covers 3–4 days)
2. Season with salt, pepper, garlic powder, paprika
3. Bake at 400°F for 22–25 minutes (internal temp 165°F)
4. Let cool, slice, store in glass containers in the fridge
5. Alternatively: use a slow cooker. Chicken + salsa on low for 4 hours. Shred.

**Sweet Potatoes:**
1. Wash 6–8 medium sweet potatoes
2. Poke holes with a fork
3. Microwave method (fastest): microwave 2 at a time for 8–10 minutes, turning halfway
4. Oven method (better taste): bake at 400°F for 45–50 minutes
5. Store in fridge. Reheat by microwave 2–3 minutes.

**Daily assembly (2 minutes):**
1. Grab container of chicken
2. Grab 2 sweet potatoes from fridge
3. Microwave 2–3 minutes
4. Mash sweet potatoes in bowl, add chicken on top, add sriracha
5. Eat

### Meal 2: Evening Default (7:00 PM)

You have two default options. Alternate between them or pick the same one every night. Doesn't matter.

**Option A: Protein Ice Cream (Ninja Creami)**
- 2 scoops protein powder (50g protein, ~240 cal) — use whatever brand you like
- 1 cup milk or almond milk (~8g protein, ~80–120 cal)
- 1 frozen banana (~100 cal, ~25g carbs)
- Blend, freeze in Creami pint, spin on "ice cream" setting

**Macro total: ~58g protein, ~420–460 cal**

**Option B: Greek Yogurt Bowl**
- 1.5 cups plain Greek yogurt (~30g protein, ~200 cal)
- 1 scoop protein powder (25g protein, ~120 cal)
- 1/2 cup blueberries (~40 cal)
- 1 tbsp honey (~60 cal)
- Mix together.

**Macro total: ~55g protein, ~420 cal**

**Option C: Leftover Chicken + Sweet Potato (smaller portion)**
- 6 oz chicken + 300g sweet potato + sriracha
- Same as Meal 1 but 75% the size

**Macro total: ~42g protein, ~510 cal**

### Protein Gaps

The two meals above give you ~100–115g of protein. You need ~200g. The gap is covered by:

| Source | When | Protein |
|---|---|---|
| Protein shake (2 scoops + water) | Post-run, with morning supplements | 50g |
| Amino Energy | Morning | ~5g |
| Protein bar or jerky | Afternoon snack if hungry | 20–30g |

**Daily total: ~175–200g protein from these sources.** You don't need to obsess over hitting 200 exactly. Consistently hitting 170+ is fine.

## 7. The AM Block — What You Actually Do

"Protect the AM block" is meaningless unless you know exactly what to do when you sit down at 8:30.

### The AM Block Startup Sequence (8:30 AM, Every Weekday)

1. Sit down at your desk
2. Open your notebook to last night's plan
3. Read Task 1
4. Set a 25-minute timer on your watch or desk clock (not your phone — phone is on DND)
5. Work on Task 1 until the timer goes off
6. Take a 5-minute break (stand up, water, stretch — NOT phone)
7. Reset the timer. Another 25 minutes on Task 1.
8. Repeat until Task 1 is done or until 10:00 AM
9. If Task 1 is done before 10:00, move to Task 2
10. At 11:00 AM: Stop. Block is over regardless of where you are. Move to comms window.

This is the Pomodoro method. 25 on, 5 off. It works because it breaks "2.5 hours of deep work" into manageable chunks. You're not doing 150 minutes straight — you're doing six 25-minute sprints.

### What Goes Into Task 1 (By Day)

Be specific the night before. Not "work on ReplyScale" — that's too vague and your brain will wander. Instead:

**Good Task 1 examples:**
- "Send 10 cold outreach messages to agencies on the ReplyScale prospect list"
- "Build the demo deck for ReplyScale — slides 1–5"
- "Run the numbers on the [specific address] property — comps, ARV, repair estimate"
- "Write the LOI for the [specific] deal and send to seller"
- "Set up the AI automation workflow for [specific client deliverable]"
- "Record 1 ReplyScale demo video for the website"

**Bad Task 1 examples:**
- "Work on business stuff" (too vague)
- "Research real estate" (research what, specifically?)
- "ReplyScale" (that's a noun, not a task)
- "Catch up on things" (this is how you end up in email for 2 hours)

### Hour Billing: How to Actually Do It

At the end of the AM block (11:00 AM) and the end of the PM block (4:00–5:00 PM):

1. Open whatever you use for time tracking (spreadsheet, app, or even a text file)
2. Log: Date, Start time, End time, What you did, Client (family office vs. ReplyScale vs. personal)
3. Example entry: `Feb 20 | 8:30–11:00 | 2.5 hrs | ReplyScale outreach + demo prep | ReplyScale`
4. Example entry: `Feb 20 | 11:30–1:30 | 2 hrs | Reviewed Q4 financials, call with [name] | Family Office`

**If you use a spreadsheet, it should have 5 columns:**

| Date | Time Block | Hours | Description | Client |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2/20 | AM | 2.5 | ReplyScale outreach + demo deck | ReplyScale |
| 2/20 | Midday | 2.0 | Q4 financials review, call w/ [name] | Family Office |
| 2/20 | PM | 1.5 | Billing, email follow-ups, deal paperwork | Family Office |

At the end of each week, sum the "Family Office" hours. That's your billable total. Multiply by $75. Send the invoice.

## 8. Financial Check — Exact Friday Routine

**When:** Friday, during your 4:00–5:00 PM admin block.
**Time required:** 5 minutes. Set a timer if you want — you'll be done before it goes off.

### Step-by-Step

1. **Open Card B app or statement** (your "Life" / discretionary card)
   - Look at one number: **total charges this billing cycle**
   - Write it down: "Week of [date]: Card B at $_____ of $3,000"
   - Is it on track? (Week 1: should be under ~$750. Week 2: under ~$1,500. Week 3: under ~$2,250.)
   - If over pace: identify the biggest expense this week. Was it DoorDash? An impulse buy? A necessary expense? Just notice it.

2. **Open Card A app or statement** (your "Fixed" card)
   - Scan for anything unexpected. Any charge you don't recognize?
   - If everything looks normal: done.
   - If something is off: flag it and deal with it next week during admin time.

3. **Check bank account balance**
   - Is it above $10,000? If yes: you're fine.
   - If no: look at incoming deposits this month. Will it come back above $10k? If not, you need to cut discretionary spend.

4. **Log the weekly number**
   - In your "Weekly Spend" spreadsheet/note: Date, Card B total, any notes
   - Example: `2/21 | Card B: $1,847 / $3,000 | On track, one DoorDash order this week`

5. **Done.** Close everything. That's it until next Friday.

### What Input Is Required From You

- Open 2–3 apps
- Look at 3 numbers
- Write 1 line
- Close apps
- 5 minutes, once a week

## 10. Mode B — Symptomatic Day, Step by Step

When you wake up and you're in a symptomatic period, don't try to figure out what to do. Just follow these steps in order.

### Mode B Morning

1. Wake up naturally (no forced alarm — sleep is recovery)
2. Drink water. Take supplements.
3. Put on shoes. Go outside.
4. **Walk for 20–30 minutes.** Not a run. Just walk. Around the block, around the neighborhood, on a treadmill if the weather is bad. Listen to a podcast, music, or nothing.
5. Come home. Shower.

### Mode B Work (1 Hour Only)

6. Open your notebook. Look at your nightly plan (you wrote one last night, right?).
7. Do Task 1 for 1 hour. Set a timer.
8. When the timer goes off, stop. Log the hour. You're done with work for the day.
9. If you can't focus for a full hour: do 30 minutes. If you can't do 30: do 15. Something > nothing.

### Mode B Meals

10. **Meal 1: chicken + sweet potato.** It's prepped. Microwave it. Eat it. Don't think about alternatives. Don't open DoorDash.
11. **Meal 2: protein ice cream or yogurt bowl.** Same as always. Default fires automatically.
12. If you genuinely can't stomach food: protein shake. 2 scoops, water, done.

### Mode B Recovery (Afternoon/Evening)

13. **Sauna (25 minutes) + Red Light Therapy.** Heat the sauna, position the red light panel, sit for 25 minutes. This is NOT optional in Mode B — it's actually more important when symptomatic. The heat supports your CIRS detox pathways and the red light aids cellular recovery. Hydrate well before and after.

### Mode B Evening

14. No guilt about watching TV, resting, or doing nothing productive. You did the minimum. That's enough.
15. **9:30 PM: Phone goes on charger outside bedroom.**
16. **Write tomorrow's Mode B plan.** Just 3 lines:
    - Walk: Y
    - Task 1: [single most important thing]
    - Sauna + red light: Y
    - Meals: chicken/sweet potato + [evening default]
17. **Bed by 10:30.**

### How Long to Stay in Mode B

Stay in Mode B until you have **2 consecutive days where you wake up and feel like you could run.** Not "I feel perfect" — just "I could probably do a 20-minute run." That's your signal to start transitioning back to Mode A.

Transition day: Run 20 minutes (not full distance). Do 1.5 hours of work instead of 1. If that goes well, next day move to Mode A.
