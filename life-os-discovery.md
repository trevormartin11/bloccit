# Life OS — Discovery Questionnaire

Answer every question honestly. Short answers are fine where appropriate, but don't skip the ones that make you uncomfortable — those are the most important ones.

---

## A. Current Daily Routine & Energy Patterns (1–10)

1. What time did you actually wake up each day this past week? (List each day.)
2. What did you do in the first 60 minutes after waking each of those days — honestly, not what you wish you did?
3. What time did you go to bed each night this past week, and what were you doing in the last hour before sleep?
4. At what time of day do you feel the sharpest and most focused? When do you hit a wall?
5. How many hours of your day would you describe as genuinely productive vs. just "busy" or wasted?
6. Do you have any consistent daily routine right now, or does every day look different? Describe what a typical weekday actually looks like hour by hour.
7. What does your weekend typically look like? Is it recovery, socializing, chores, or more of the same?
8. How often do you eat meals at consistent times? Do you skip meals or eat at your desk?
9. How many cups of coffee or caffeinated drinks do you have per day, and when?
10. If you had to rate your average daily energy from 1–10 over the past month, what would it be? What drags it down the most?

---

## B. Health — Physical, Mental, Sleep, Nutrition (11–22)

11. How many hours of sleep do you actually get on average? Do you wake up feeling rested or groggy?
12. Do you have trouble falling asleep, staying asleep, or both? Do you use your phone in bed?
13. What does your diet actually look like? Describe what you ate yesterday in full.
14. How many servings of vegetables and fruit do you eat on a typical day? Be honest.
15. How much water do you drink daily? Do you track it?
16. Do you take any supplements or medications? List them.
17. Do you have any diagnosed health conditions, chronic pain, or recurring issues you're managing or ignoring?
18. When was your last physical, blood panel, or dentist visit?
19. Do you drink alcohol? If so, how many drinks per week on average?
20. Do you use nicotine, cannabis, or any other substances? How often?
21. How would you rate your mental health right now on a 1–10 scale? What's the main thing pulling it down?
22. Are you currently seeing a therapist, counselor, or psychiatrist? If not, do you think you should be?

---

## C. Fitness & Body Composition (23–33)

23. What is your current height, weight, and approximate body fat percentage (if known)?
24. What is your target weight or body composition, and by when?
25. What does your current exercise routine look like? How many days per week, what type, and for how long?
26. If you don't exercise consistently, what specifically stops you? Be specific — not "motivation," but what actually happens in the moment you skip it.
27. Do you have a gym membership, home gym, or neither? What equipment do you have access to?
28. Have you ever followed a structured training program? Which ones, and what happened?
29. Do you have any injuries or physical limitations that affect training?
30. What's your current level of cardiovascular fitness? Could you run a mile right now without stopping?
31. How much do you sit per day? Do you have a standing desk or take movement breaks?
32. What's the longest streak you've maintained with a fitness routine, and what broke it?
33. Do you track workouts, steps, or any fitness metrics? What tools or apps do you use?

---

## D. Work & Career (34–45)

34. What do you do for work? Describe your role, responsibilities, and how long you've been in it.
35. Are you employed, self-employed, freelancing, between jobs, or building something on the side?
36. What does "career success" look like to you in concrete terms — title, income, autonomy, impact? Be specific.
37. Where are you right now relative to that vision? What percentage of the way there?
38. What is the single biggest blocker to your career progress right now?
39. How many hours per week do you actually work (not just "at work")?
40. How much of your work time is spent on high-leverage activities vs. busywork, meetings, or distractions?
41. Do you have a side project, business idea, or secondary income stream you're working on or want to start?
42. What skills do you know you need to develop but keep putting off?
43. Do you have a mentor, coach, or peer group that pushes you professionally?
44. What's your relationship with your boss or clients like? Is it helping or hurting your growth?
45. If you could redesign your work life from scratch with no constraints, what would it look like?

---

## E. Finances (46–57)

46. What is your current annual income (or monthly, if variable)?
47. What is your monthly take-home pay after taxes?
48. What are your fixed monthly expenses (rent, utilities, subscriptions, insurance, debt payments)? List them.
49. How much do you spend per month on discretionary things (eating out, shopping, entertainment)? Do you actually know this number?
50. Do you have a budget? If so, do you follow it? If not, why not?
51. How much do you have in savings right now? Is that an emergency fund or just what's left over?
52. Do you have any debt? List each one with the balance, interest rate, and minimum payment.
53. What is your current net worth (assets minus liabilities), roughly?
54. What net worth or income target are you aiming for, and by when?
55. Are you investing? If so, where (401k, IRA, brokerage, crypto, real estate)? How much per month?
56. What's the dumbest recurring expense you keep paying but never use?
57. If you lost your income tomorrow, how many months could you survive without changing your lifestyle?

---

## F. Relationships (58–67)

58. Are you in a romantic relationship? If so, how would you rate it from 1–10 and why?
59. If single, is finding a partner a priority? What's actually preventing it — time, effort, confidence, or something else?
60. How often do you see or talk to your closest friends? Has that frequency changed in the past year?
61. How many people in your life would you call if you had a genuine emergency at 2 AM? List the number, not the names.
62. What's your relationship like with your family? Are there unresolved tensions affecting you?
63. Do you feel socially isolated, even if you technically "have friends"?
64. When was the last time you had a meaningful, honest conversation with someone — not small talk?
65. Are there relationships in your life that drain you but you haven't set boundaries around?
66. Do you initiate plans with people, or do you wait to be invited? How often do you cancel?
67. If your closest friend described your reliability and follow-through as a person, what would they honestly say?

---

## G. Living Environment (68–73)

68. Describe your living situation — do you own or rent, live alone or with others, and are you satisfied with it?
69. On a scale of 1–10, how clean and organized is your living space right now?
70. Are there maintenance tasks, repairs, or organizational projects you've been putting off? List them.
71. Does your physical environment support or sabotage your goals? (e.g., junk food in the kitchen, no dedicated workspace, cluttered desk)
72. What does your workspace look like? Do you have a setup that supports focused work?
73. If someone walked into your home unannounced right now, would you be embarrassed? Why?

---

## H. Personal Development & Learning (74–80)

74. What's the last book you finished? When?
75. How many books have you read in the past 12 months? How many did you start but not finish?
76. Are there specific skills you want to learn or improve? List them in order of priority.
77. How do you currently learn — courses, YouTube, books, podcasts, mentors, trial and error?
78. How much time per week do you spend on intentional learning vs. passive content consumption (scrolling, random YouTube)?
79. Do you have any creative hobbies or outlets? When was the last time you engaged with them?
80. What's something you used to be passionate about but dropped? Why did you stop?

---

## I. Time Management & Productivity (81–88)

81. If you tracked every minute of your day for a week, what would be the biggest time sink you'd be ashamed of?
82. How much time per day do you spend on your phone? What's your screen time report say? Which apps dominate?
83. How much time do you spend on social media daily? Which platforms?
84. Do you use any productivity system (to-do lists, time blocking, Pomodoro, GTD, etc.)? Does it actually work for you?
85. How many tabs and apps do you have open right now? Do you close things when you're done or let them pile up?
86. How often do you context-switch during focused work? Do notifications pull you out?
87. Do you plan your day the night before or the morning of, or not at all?
88. When you have a free hour with no obligations, what do you actually do with it vs. what you wish you'd do?

---

## J. Habits — Bad Ones to Kill, Good Ones to Build (89–94)

89. List every habit you want to eliminate. Be specific — not "eat better" but "stop ordering Uber Eats at 11 PM three times a week."
90. List every habit you want to build. Same level of specificity.
91. What's the habit you've tried and failed to build the most times? What happened each time?
92. What triggers your worst habits? (Boredom, stress, loneliness, specific times of day, specific environments?)
93. Do you use any habit tracking apps or methods? Have they worked?
94. What's one small habit you successfully built in the past? How did you do it?

---

## K. Motivation, Accountability & Past Failures (95–100)

95. What type of motivation actually works on you — fear of consequences, rewards, competition, public accountability, internal drive, or something else?
96. What systems, apps, coaches, or programs have you tried before to get your life together? Why did each one fail?
97. When you fall off a system, what's the typical pattern? Do you abandon it all at once, or does it erode gradually?
98. Do you perform better with external accountability (someone checking on you) or internal discipline? Be honest about which one you actually respond to, not which one you wish worked.
99. What are your non-negotiable values — the things you'd refuse to compromise on even if it cost you? List at most five.
100. Describe the specific life you want to be living in 1 year, 5 years, and 10 years. Not vague aspirations — what does a Tuesday look like? Where do you live? What's in your bank account? Who's next to you? What did you do that morning?

## How to Use This

1. Copy this file or open it in your editor.
2. Write your answers directly below each question.
3. Don't overthink it — first honest answer is usually the right one.
4. Paste the completed answers back. We'll use them to architect your full Life OS system.
